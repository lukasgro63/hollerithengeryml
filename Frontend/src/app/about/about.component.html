About

HollerithEnergyML is a project developed as part of the Master's program in Digital Business Engineering (M.Sc.) at the Hermann-Hollerit-Center, in collaboration with the KI Lab Region Stuttgart. Our aim is to investigate the energy consumption of machine learning models and find ways to make their use more efficient.

We conducted a detailed analysis of various machine learning models to compare their resource consumption. This analysis led to the development of a predictive model capable of estimating the energy needed for specific tasks, based on the number of numerical and categorical features and the size of the datasets.

As a pivotal outcome of our work, we introduce the HollerithEnergyML Recommender System. This system guides users towards more energy-efficient practices in machine learning training, aligning with our broader goal of fostering sustainable development in artificial intelligence.
